Part III: THE DREAM IS GOING DOWN

In December 2000 the FAA approved the airport project. To the Town, this blessing seemed to safeguard against any mistakes made while drafting the development agreement.

But not everyone adored the FAA’s decision. Conservationist organizations grew concerned. An expanded airport meant potentially tens of thousands more visitors to the Eastern Sierras, which would leave an unknown tread on the delicate environment.

In Manning's opinion, these groups were simply anti-development. “They felt that if they could stop the airport that would stop growth in town,” he said.

The state of California and conservationist organizations sued the US Department of Transportation, including the FAA, for approving the project. They argued the Town never fully considered what doubling their population would do to the environment.

The judge agreed. He ruled that Mammoth was prohibited “from commencing any construction or other work on the airport expansion project.” That is, unless they complied with all regulations.

This meant the Town would have to start the process over. Old problems would soon reemerge.
