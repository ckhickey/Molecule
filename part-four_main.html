Part IV: THE RECKONING

Guilty.

A sense of disbelief took over the town after the ruling. Many people blamed the jury, chosen from a rural area outside of Mammoth Lakes, for taking revenge through the court case.

A piece of land around the airport stood vacant, and now the town owed tens of millions of dollars to settle. But the judgment also took into consideration prospective profits from Ballas' agreement. In 2010, the Court of Appeal of the State of California Third Appellate District affirmed the judgment against the town.

It all came to a head in the spring of 2012.
